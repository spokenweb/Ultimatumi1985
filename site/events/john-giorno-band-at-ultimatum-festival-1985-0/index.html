The Ultimatum Festival I, May 1985

John Giorno Band at Ultimatum Festival 1985-05-05

The following is a transcription of the John Giorno Band’s performance at the Ultimatum Festival I, Sunday, May 5th, 1985 at the Foufounes Electriques bar and performance space in Montreal. John Giorno was an American poet, performance artist and activist who first rose to prominence through his collaborations with Andy Warhol. According to Poetry Foundation, he is known for his innovative poetry recordings in which he used synthesizers and distortion to create what he called "electronic sensory poetry environments". Giorno and his band were the headliners for Ultimatum I. In Alan Lord’s memoir High Friends in Low Places (2022) he writes: "The festival climax on the last night was of course our big star John Giorno and his band. He looked real sharp in a white jacket, like a postmodern Tony Bennett. He gave us his classic gems such as (Last Night) I Gambled With My Anger And Lost, and Scum and Slime… and Stretching it Wider".
